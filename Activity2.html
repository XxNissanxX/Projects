Pizza is an Italian dish of a flattened dough base topped with sauce, cheese, and various other ingredients, baked at a high temperature. Originating in Naples, the modern pizza emerged in the 18th century as a food for the working class, but it gained global popularity after Italian immigrants brought it to the United States. A famous variety, the Margherita pizza, features tomato, mozzarella, and basil and is said to have been named after Queen Margherita of Savoy.
